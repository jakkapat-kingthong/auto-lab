# Gaze Heads: How VLMs Look at What They Describe

> ArXiv | 2026-06-15 | paper 1

How a vision-language model internally solves the task of describing an image is far from obvious. We find that the model develops a specific mechanism for this: a small set of attention heads in its language-model backbone, which we call gaze heads, whose attention tracks the image region the model is currently describing.
